Why I choose Vim as my editor

My classmates are often confused with my terminal lifestyle, so I dedicate this small article to them, explaining my views.

Pros:

  • If you really know what you want to do and how, Vim won't hold you back
  • Programmable text-editing
  • The simplicity of the interface gives it limitless expandability.
  • Constantly developed by millions of great devs worldwide.
  • Open-source, if you don't like something, change it! (VS 2010, I'm looking at you)

Cons:

  • Steep learning curve (months/years)
  • Too many commands at first
  • let girlfriendcount = 0

I advise you to use Vim if you:

  • are serious about development in long-term
  • want to write more in less time
  • are tired of using the mouse
  • love learning things, that are hard, but after you master them, give you god-like powers.
If you are still not convinced:
$~: vimtutor

My favourite Vim plugins:

Snipmate,Ctags,syntastic,vim-powerline

Useful not-so-well-known commands:

Indent current file properly: gg(Shift+V)G=
Insert newline over the current and switch to Insert mode: O
Move to last character on the current line and switch to Insert mode: A
